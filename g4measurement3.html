What Are a.m. and p.m.?
a.m. (Ante Meridiem):
a.m. means before noon. It’s used for times from midnight (12:00 midnight) to just before 12:00 noon.
Example: If it's 8:00 a.m., it’s in the morning—8:00 after midnight but before noon.

p.m. (Post Meridiem):
p.m. means after noon. It’s used for times from 12:00 noon to just before midnight (12:00 midnight).
Example: If it’s 3:00 p.m., it’s in the afternoon—3:00 after 12:00 noon.

How to Use the 12-Hour Clock
The 12-hour clock is used to tell time with two sets of 12 hours—one for the morning (a.m.) and one for the afternoon/evening (p.m.). Instead of 24 hours in a day (like the 24-hour clock), we split the day into two 12-hour periods.

How it works:
12:00 a.m. is midnight.
12:00 p.m. is noon.
Times like 6:00 a.m. and 6:00 p.m. are used to show the difference between morning and evening.

Examples of Morning (a.m.) Times
Morning (a.m.):

10:00 a.m. → It’s 10 o'clock in the morning, before noon.
