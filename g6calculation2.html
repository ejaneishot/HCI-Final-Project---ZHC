Place Value in Multiplication and Division

What is Place Value?
Place value tells us the value of a digit based on its position in a number.

Example:
  • In 450, the 4 is in the hundreds place (400), and the 5 is in the tens place (50).
Using Place Value to Multiply
Step 1: Break the Number into Parts:
Multiply each part separately, then add the results.
Example:
  • 23 × 4: Break 23 into 20 and 3.
  • Multiply: (20 × 4) + (3 × 4) = 80 + 12 = 92.

Step 2: Multiply by 10, 100, or 1000:
Add zeros to the number based on the factor.
Example:
  • 25 × 100 = 2500.

Step 3: Use Known Facts:
If you know 6 × 7 = 42, then 60 × 7 = 420 (use place value).

Using Place Value to Divide
Step 1: Break the Number into Parts:
Divide each part separately.
Example:
  • 84 ÷ 4: Break 84 into 80 and 4.
  • Divide: (80 ÷ 4) + (4 ÷ 4) = 20 + 1 = 21.

Step 2: Divide by 10, 100, or 1000:
Move the decimal point to the left based on the factor.
Example:
  • 250 ÷ 10 = 25, 3400 ÷ 100 = 34.

Step 3: Use Known Facts:
If you know 36 ÷ 6 = 6, then 360 ÷ 6 = 60 (use place value).
