Place Value in Whole Numbers
Place value tells you the value of each digit in a number based on where it is.

Example:
In the number 4,572, the place value of each digit is:
  • 4 is in the thousands place (4,000)
  • 5 is in the hundreds place (500)
  • 7 is in the tens place (70)
  • 2 is in the ones place (2)

Larger numbers:
In 53,689, the place values are:
  • 5 in the ten thousands (50,000)
  • 3 in the thousands (3,000)
  • 6 in the hundreds (600)
  • 8 in the tens (80)
  • 9 in the ones (9)

Place Value in Decimals
In decimals, the place values start after the decimal point.

Example:
In 4.38:
  • 4 is in the units place (4)
  • 3 is in the tenths place (0.3)
  • 8 is in the hundredths place (0.08)

In 0.543:
  • 5 is in the tenths place (0.5)
  • 4 is in the hundredths place (0.04)
  • 3 is in the thousandths place (0.003)
