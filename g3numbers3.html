Rounding Numbers to the Nearest 10 or 100

Let’s Learn How to Round Numbers to the Nearest 10 or 100!
Rounding means changing a number to make it simpler but still close to the original number. When we round to the nearest 10 or 100, we look at the digits and decide which 10 or 100 it’s closest to.
How to Round to the Nearest 10

Look at the ones place:

  • If the ones digit is 5 or more, round up to the next ten.
  • If the ones digit is 4 or less, round down to the previous ten.

Examples:
  • Round 73 to the nearest 10: Look at the ones place (3). Since 3 is less than 5, round down to 70.
  • Round 78 to the nearest 10: Look at the ones place (8). Since 8 is 5 or more, round up to 80.

How to Round to the Nearest 100

Look at the tens place:

  • If the tens digit is 5 or more, round up to the next hundred.
  • If the tens digit is 4 or less, round down to the previous hundred.

Examples:
  • Round 453 to the nearest 100: Look at the tens place (5). Since 5 is 5 or more, round up to 500.
  • Round 463 to the nearest 100: Look at the tens place (6). Since 6 is 5 or more, round up to 500.
