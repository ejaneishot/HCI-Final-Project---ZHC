Doubles and Halves of Numbers

What are Doubles?
Doubles:
When you add a number to itself, or multiply it by 2, you get its double.

Example:
Double of 8 is 8 + 8 = 16, or 8 × 2 = 16.

What are Halves?
Halves:
When you divide a number into 2 equal parts, you get its half.

Example:
Half of 16 is 16 ÷ 2 = 8.

Finding Doubles and Halves for Numbers Up to 50
For numbers up to 50:

Doubles:
24 × 2 = 48 (Double of 24)
32 × 2 = 64 (Double of 32)
45 × 2 = 90 (Double of 45)

Halves:
24 ÷ 2 = 12 (Half of 24)
32 ÷ 2 = 16 (Half of 32)
45 ÷ 2 = 22.5 (Half of 45)

Doubles and Halves for Multiples of 10 and 100 up to 5000
For multiples of 10 or 100 up to 5000:

Doubles:
Double of 500 = 500 + 500 = 1000
Double of 1000 = 1000 + 1000 = 2000
Double of 2000 = 2000 + 2000 = 4000

Halves:
Half of 500 = 500 ÷ 2 = 250
Half of 1000 = 1000 ÷ 2 = 500
Half of 2000 = 2000 ÷ 2 = 1000
