Operations with Large Numbers and Decimals

Let’s learn how to add, subtract, multiply, and divide large numbers and decimals!

These are important skills to solve more complex problems involving large numbers and decimals efficiently.

Adding Large Numbers and Decimals
Step 1: Align the Numbers:

Write the numbers in a column, aligning the digits by place value (ones, tens, etc.). For decimals, align the decimal points.

Step 2: Add from Right to Left:

Start adding from the rightmost digit and carry over if the sum is more than 9.

Example:

  • 123.45 + 67.8 = 191.25

Quiz: Add the Following Numbers

1. What is 456.78 + 123.45?
Subtracting Large Numbers and Decimals
Step 1: Align the Numbers:

Write the numbers in a column, aligning the digits and decimal points.

Step 2: Subtract from Right to Left:

Start from the rightmost digit, borrowing from the next column if needed.

Example:

  • 200.5 - 76.3 = 124.2

Quiz: Subtract the Following Numbers

1. What is 300.00 - 123.45?
Multiplying Large Numbers and Decimals
Step 1: Ignore the Decimals at First:

Multiply the numbers as if they are whole numbers.

Step 2: Place the Decimal Point:

Count the total number of decimal places in the original numbers. Add that many decimal places to your answer.

Example:

  • 12.3 × 4.5 = 55.35

Quiz: Multiply the Following Numbers

1. What is 5.5 × 6?
Dividing Large Numbers and Decimals
Step 1: For Decimals in the Divisor:

If dividing by a decimal, move the decimal point in the divisor (the number you are dividing by) to make it a whole number, and do the same for the dividend (the number being divided).

Step 2: Divide as Usual:

Perform long division, keeping the decimal point in the same place as in the adjusted dividend.

Example:

  • 56.7 ÷ 3 = 18.9

Quiz: Divide the Following Numbers

1. What is 72 ÷ 8?
