Geometry 101

2D Shapes:

2D Shapes are flat shapes. They don’t have height or depth, just length and width. You can draw or see them on paper or a screen.

Circle:

A circle is round with no corners or sides.
Example: a clock is a circle!

Identify the circle

Square:

A square has 4 sides that are all the same length, and 4 corners that are sharp.
Example: a box is a square!

Identify the square

Rectangle:

A rectangle has 4 sides. The opposite sides are the same length, but the sides are not all equal.
Example: a book is a rectangle!

Identify the rectangle

Triangle:

A triangle has 3 sides and 3 corners.
Example: a slice of pizza is a triangle!

Identify the triangle

Symmetry:

Symmetry means that a shape looks the same on both sides when you fold it or divide it into two parts.

Example: If you fold a paper in half and both sides match exactly, that’s symmetry!
A butterfly has symmetry. If you fold it in half, both sides look the same!

How to recognize symmetry:

To recognize if a shape is symmetric, you will need to find the middle of the shape and fold it down the middle to see if both sides match. If they do, the shape is symmetrical.
