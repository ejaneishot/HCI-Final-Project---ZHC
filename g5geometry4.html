Calculating Angles on a Straight Line

Let’s learn how to calculate angles on a straight line!
The total of all the angles on a straight line is always 180°. This is because a straight line creates a 180° angle.

If two angles are on a straight line, their sum will always be 180°.
What does “Angles on a Straight Line” Mean?
The total of all the angles on a straight line is 180°. If you have two angles that are on a straight line, the sum of those two angles will always be 180°.

Example: If one angle is 110° on a straight line, the other angle must be:

180° - 110° = 70°
How to Calculate Angles on a Straight Line
Follow these steps to calculate angles on a straight line:
  1. Step 1: Identify the given angle on the straight line.
  2. Step 2: Subtract the given angle from 180° to find the other angle.
  3. Step 3: Check if the sum of the two angles equals 180°.
Example: If the given angle is 110°, subtract it from 180°:

180° - 110° = 70°

So, the other angle is 70°.
