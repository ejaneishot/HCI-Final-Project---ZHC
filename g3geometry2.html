Classifying shapes means putting them into groups based on their features, like the number of sides, corners, or angles.
Classifying 2D Shapes by Sides
Shapes can be classified by the number of sides:

A triangle has 3 sides, a square has 4 sides, and a pentagon has 5 sides. The more sides, the more complex the shape.

Examples:
  • Square: 4 sides
  • Triangle: 3 sides
  • Hexagon: 6 sides

Classifying 2D Shapes by Vertices
Shapes can be classified by the number of vertices (corners):

The number of corners where the sides meet is called vertices. A square has 4 vertices, while a triangle has 3 vertices.

Examples:
  • Square: 4 vertices
  • Triangle: 3 vertices
  • Octagon: 8 vertices

Classifying 2D Shapes by Right Angles
Right Angles: A right angle is an L-shaped corner (90 degrees). Shapes like squares and rectangles have right angles.

Examples:
  • Square: 4 right angles
  • Rectangle: 4 right angles
  • Triangle: 1 right angle (right triangle)
