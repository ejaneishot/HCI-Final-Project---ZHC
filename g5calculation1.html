Decimal pairs are two numbers that add up to a specific value, such as 1 or 10.
Decimal Pairs Totaling 1
  • 0.5 + 0.5 = 1
  • 0.8 + 0.2 = 1
  • 0.7 + 0.3 = 1
  • 0.9 + 0.1 = 1
How to find pairs:
  • If you know one decimal, subtract it from 1 to find the other decimal.
  • Example: If you have 0.6, subtract it from 1:
    • 1 - 0.6 = 0.4, so 0.6 + 0.4 = 1.

Decimal Pairs Totaling 10
  • 5 + 5 = 10
  • 7.3 + 2.7 = 10
  • 8.6 + 1.4 = 10
  • 9.2 + 0.8 = 10
How to find pairs:
  • If you know one decimal, subtract it from 10 to find the other decimal.
  • Example: If you have 6.4, subtract it from 10:
    • 10 - 6.4 = 3.6, so 6.4 + 3.6 = 10.
