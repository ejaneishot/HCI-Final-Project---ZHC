How to Compare Whole Numbers
  • To compare whole numbers, look at the leftmost digits.
  • The larger number is the one with the bigger digit in that place.
Example: 50 is greater than 30 because 5 is bigger than 3.

How to Compare Negative Numbers
  • For negative numbers, the smaller the number, the more negative it is.
  • The number farther from zero is smaller.
Example: -7 is less than -3 because -7 is farther from zero.

How to Compare Decimal Numbers
  • To compare decimals, start from the left and compare each place value (tenths, hundredths, thousandths).
  • The number with the higher digit in the first place that differs is greater.
Example: 3.56 is greater than 3.45 because 5 is greater than 4 in the hundredths place.
