What does it mean to Partition Numbers?
Partitioning a number means breaking it into smaller parts based on place value.

Example:
For the number 5,432, we can partition it as: 5,000 + 400 + 30 + 2. This helps us understand each part of the number based on its place (thousands, hundreds, tens, ones).

What is Decimal Notation?
Decimal notation is how we write numbers that include parts of a whole.

Example:
3.5 means 3 whole and 5 tenths (0.5).
12.75 means 12 whole and 75 hundredths (0.75).

How to Use Decimal Notation
Decimal Places: The first digit after the decimal is the tenths place. The second digit is the hundredths place. The third digit is the thousandths place, and so on.

Example:
Reading decimals: 7.32 means 7 whole and 32 hundredths.
Partitioning decimals: For 5.67, we can partition it as 5 whole and 0.67 (which is 6 tenths and 7 hundredths).
