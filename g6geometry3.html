Measuring Angles and Verifying the 180° Sum in Triangles

Measuring Angles
Angles are formed where two lines meet at a point.
Use a protractor to measure angles in degrees (°).

Steps to Measure Angles:
  1. Place the center hole of the protractor on the vertex of the angle.
  2. Line up one side of the angle with the 0° line of the protractor.
  3. Read the measurement on the protractor where the other side of the angle points.

Sum of Angles in a Triangle
The sum of the interior angles of any triangle is always 180°.

Example: If you have a triangle with angles of 60°, 70°, and 50°, their sum should be:
60° + 70° + 50° = 180°.

This is true for all triangles, no matter their size or shape.

How to Verify the 180° Sum
Steps to Verify the 180° Sum:
  1. Measure the three angles of a triangle using a protractor.
  2. Add the three angles together.
  3. Check that the total is 180°.
