Work with Fractions and Their Equivalents

Let’s Learn About Fractions and Their Equivalents!
Fractions are parts of a whole. When you divide something into equal parts, each part is called a fraction.
What Are Fractions?
Fractions are parts of a whole. When something is divided into equal parts, each part is a fraction.

Examples:
  • ½ (half): If you cut a pizza into 2 equal parts, each part is one-half.
  • ¼ (quarter): If you cut a sandwich into 4 equal parts, each part is one-quarter.
  • ¾ (three-quarters): If you take 3 out of 4 equal parts, you have three-quarters.
What Are Equivalent Fractions?
Equivalent fractions are fractions that are equal, even if they look different.

Examples:
  • ½ = ²/₄: One-half is the same as two-quarters because they cover the same amount.
  • ¼ = ²/₈: One-quarter is the same as two-eighths.
How to Work with Fractions
Identifying Fractions: Look at how many parts a whole is divided into and how many parts you’re counting.

Example: If a shape is divided into 4 parts and 1 part is shaded, it’s ¼.


Finding Equivalents: You can find equivalent fractions by multiplying or dividing the top and bottom numbers of the fraction.

Example: If you double the top and bottom numbers of ½, you get ²/₄, which is still the same amount.
