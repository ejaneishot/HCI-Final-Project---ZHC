Measurement

Length
Length tells us how long or short something is.

Comparing lengths: We can compare how long two things are by using our eyes or hands. For example, we can say this pencil is longer than this crayon.

Vocabulary: Shorter, Longer
Using Non-Standard Units
We can use objects like blocks or your hand to measure things. For example, this string is 5 blocks long.
Mass
Mass tells us how heavy or light something is.

Comparing mass: We can compare how heavy two things are by holding them. For example, this rock is heavier than this leaf.

Vocabulary: Heavier, Lighter
Using Non-Standard Units for Mass
We can use things like toy cars or blocks to measure weight. For example, this apple weighs as much as 3 toy cars.
Read Time to the Hour
A clock is a device that helps us tell the time. It has two hands: the long hand and the short hand.

Long hand: the minute hand. It helps us tell the minute.
Short hand: the hour hand. It helps us tell the hour.

How to read the time to the hour:
Look at the long hand (minute hand), if it is pointed to 12, it’s o’clock.
Look at the short hand (hour hand), the short hand tells you the hour.
Say the time, if the short hand is pointed to 5 for example and the long hand is pointed to 12, you say it’s 5 o’clock.
Units of Time
Time helps us know when things happen, like when to eat breakfast, go to school, or play with friends.

There are many different units of time:
Minutes: A minute is a very short amount of time. It’s how long it might take to tie your shoes.
Example: We can count 60 seconds, and that makes 1 minute.

Hours: An hour is longer than a minute. A clock shows us hours. There are 24 hours in one day.
Example: One hour is about how long it takes to watch a cartoon.

Days: A day is the time from when you wake up in the morning until you go to bed at night.
Example: There are 24 hours in a day.

Weeks: A week is 7 days long. It starts on Sunday and ends on Saturday.
Example: We go to school for 5 days in a week, and we have 2 days off on the weekend.

Months: A month is longer than a week. It takes about 4 weeks.
Example: There are 12 months in a year. Your birthday happens in one of the months!

Years: A year is 12 months long. It’s how long it takes for the Earth to go all the way around the sun.
Example: If your last birthday was in January, you have to wait a year to celebrate your next birthday.
