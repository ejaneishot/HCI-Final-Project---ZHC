Subtract single digit numbers and double digit numbers

What is subtraction?
Subtraction means taking something away or finding how much is left. We use the - sign to show subtraction, and the answer is called the difference.
How to subtract single digit numbers:
  • Start with the first number
  • Count backwards the amount of times mentioned in the 2nd number
  • Example: 8 - 3 we should start at 8 then count back 3 times: 8, 7, 6, 5. The answer is 5!
How to subtract double digit numbers (without borrowing):
  • Subtract the tens first: for 34 - 12, start with the tens: 30 - 10 = 20.
  • Subtract the ones next: 4 - 2 = 2.
  • Put it together: 20 + 2 = 22. So, 34 - 12 = 22.
How to subtract double digit numbers (with borrowing):
  • Start with the ones place: For 43 - 27, look at the ones first: 3 - 7. You canâ€™t take 7 away from 3, so you need to borrow 1 from the tens.
  • Borrow from the tens: The 4 in the tens place becomes 3, and the 3 in the ones place becomes 13.
  • Subtract the ones: 13 - 7 = 6.
  • Subtract the tens: 3 - 2 = 1.
  • Put it together: 43 - 27 = 16.
