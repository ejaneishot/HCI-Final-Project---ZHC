Use Fractions, Ratios, and Percentages to Solve Problems

Let’s explore how to use fractions, ratios, and percentages to solve real-life problems!
These tools help us compare quantities and break down complex problems into simpler parts.
Using Fractions to Solve Problems
  • Fractions show how much of a whole something is.
  • For example, 3/4 means 3 out of 4 equal parts.
Example 1:
  • You have a pizza cut into 8 slices, and you eat 3 slices. What fraction of the pizza did you eat?
  • Answer: You ate 3/8 of the pizza.
Example 2:
  • You have 12 apples, and you give 1/4 of them to a friend. How many apples did you give away?
  • Solution: 1/4 of 12 = 12 × 1/4 = 3 apples.

Using Ratios to Solve Problems
  • Ratios tell us how two things compare.
  • For example, a ratio of 2:3 means for every 2 of one thing, there are 3 of another.
Example 1:
  • You have a bag with 6 red marbles and 9 blue marbles. What is the ratio of red to blue marbles?
  • Answer: The ratio of red to blue is 6:9, which simplifies to 2:3.
Example 2:
  • In a class, the ratio of boys to girls is 3:4. If there are 15 boys, how many girls are there?
  • Solution: Set up a proportion: 3/4 = 15/x
  • 3x = 4 × 15
  • 3x = 60
  • x = 20 girls.

Using Percentages to Solve Problems
  • Percentages express a part of 100.
  • For example, 25% means 25 out of 100.
Example 1:
  • You scored 80 out of 100 on a test. What percentage did you score?
  • Answer: (80/100) × 100 = 80%.
Example 2:
  • There are 30 students in a class, and 10 are girls. What percentage of the class are girls?
  • Solution: (10/30) × 100 = 33.33%.
