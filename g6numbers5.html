Factors, Multiples, Odd/Even Numbers, Prime Numbers, and Ratios

Let’s learn about factors, multiples, odd/even numbers, prime numbers, and ratios!
These concepts are fundamental in math and help us understand the relationships between numbers.
Factors
A factor is a number that divides evenly into another number.
  • Example: Factors of 12: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12.
  • Why? Because 12 ÷ 1 = 12, 12 ÷ 2 = 6, 12 ÷ 3 = 4, 12 ÷ 4 = 3, 12 ÷ 6 = 2, 12 ÷ 12 = 1.

Multiples
A multiple is a number you get by multiplying a number by an integer (whole number).
  • Example: Multiples of 4: 4, 8, 12, 16, 20, 24…
  • These numbers are all the result of multiplying 4 by 1, 2, 3, 4, etc.

An even number is divisible by 2. An odd number is not.
  • Example: 2, 4, 6 (even) and 1, 3, 5 (odd).

A prime number has exactly two distinct positive divisors: 1 and itself.
