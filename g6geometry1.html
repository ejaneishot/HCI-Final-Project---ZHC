Classifying Polygons, Quadrilaterals, and 3D Shapes

Classifying Polygons
What are Polygons?
Polygons are closed 2D shapes with straight sides.

Examples:
  • Triangle
  • Square
  • Pentagon
  • Hexagon
Non-Examples:
  • Shapes with curves (like circles)
  • Shapes with gaps
Classifying Quadrilaterals
Quadrilaterals are polygons with 4 sides.
Common types of quadrilaterals include:
  • Square: 4 equal sides, all right angles.
  • Rectangle: Opposite sides equal, all right angles.
  • Parallelogram: Opposite sides parallel and equal, angles may not be right.
  • Rhombus: 4 equal sides, opposite angles equal.
  • Trapezium: At least one pair of parallel sides.

Identifying 3D Shapes
3D Shapes have length, width, and height.

Properties to identify 3D shapes:
  • Faces: Flat surfaces.
  • Edges: Lines where two faces meet.
  • Vertices: Points where edges meet.

Identifying a Cube
Cube:
A cube has the following properties:
  • 6 square faces
  • 12 edges
  • 8 vertices
Identifying a Cylinder
Cylinder:
A cylinder has the following properties:
  • 2 circular faces
  • 1 curved face
  • No vertices
Identifying a Pyramid
Pyramid:
A pyramid has the following properties:
  • Triangular faces meeting at a vertex
  • 1 base
