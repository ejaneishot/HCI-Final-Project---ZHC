Comparing Fractions
Finding a Common Denominator: To compare fractions, find the least common denominator (LCD) and rewrite the fractions.
  • Example: Compare 1/4 and 2/5. The LCD of 4 and 5 is 20.
  • 1/4 = 5/20, 2/5 = 8/20
  • Answer: 2/5 is greater than 1/4.

Simple Fraction Comparison: If the fractions have the same denominator, compare the numerators.
  • Example: Compare 3/8 and 5/8. Answer: 5/8 is greater than 3/8.

Comparing Decimals
Place Value Comparison: Compare decimals by looking at the place values from left to right.
  • Example: Compare 3.47 and 3.56. Answer: 3.56 is greater because 5 (tenths) is greater than 4.

Converting Fractions to Decimals: Convert fractions to decimals for easier comparison.
  • Example: 1/2 = 0.5 and 3/4 = 0.75. Answer: 3/4 (0.75) is greater than 1/2 (0.5).

Comparing Percentages
Converting to a Common Percentage: Sometimes, we convert fractions or decimals to percentages for easy comparison.
  • Example: Compare 25% and 30%. Answer: 30% is greater.
