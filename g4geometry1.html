Lines of Symmetry

Understanding Symmetry
What is symmetry?
Symmetry means that a shape can be folded in half and the two halves look exactly the same.

Line of Symmetry:
A line of symmetry is the line where you can fold a shape so both sides match perfectly.

Finding Lines of Symmetry
How to find and draw lines of symmetry:

Look for equal halves: To find a line of symmetry, look for where you can split the shape into two equal parts that look the same.

Finding Lines of Symmetry
Example 1 (Circle):
A circle has many lines of symmetry because you can fold it in different ways and both sides will match.
You can draw lines through the center in many directions (vertical, horizontal, diagonal) to show its symmetry.

Finding Lines of Symmetry
Example 2 (Square):
A square has 4 lines of symmetry: two vertical, one horizontal, and two diagonals.
You can fold it vertically, horizontally, or diagonally to see the matching halves.

Finding Lines of Symmetry
Example 3 (Regular Triangle):
An equilateral triangle has 1 line of symmetry going down the middle from the top to the base.
