3D shapes are solid shapes with length, width, and height, like cubes, pyramids, and prisms. They are different from flat 2D shapes that only have length and width.
Pyramid
A pyramid has a flat base (like a square or triangle) and triangular faces that meet at a point at the top.

Features:
  • Faces: Flat triangles and one base.
  • Vertices: The points where the edges meet.
  • Edges: The lines where the faces meet.

Prism
A prism has two identical flat shapes at the ends, with straight sides connecting them.

Features:
  • Faces: Two flat ends and rectangular sides.
  • Vertices: Where the corners of the faces meet.
  • Edges: The lines where the sides connect.

Nets of 3D Shapes
A net is a flat 2D shape that you can fold to make a 3D shape.

Examples:
  • Cube Net: Draw 6 squares connected in a cross shape. Cut it out and fold it to make a cube.
  • Pyramid Net: Draw a square and 4 triangles around it. Cut it out and fold it to make a pyramid.
