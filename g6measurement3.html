Perimeter and Area Calculations

Perimeter
The perimeter is the total distance around the outside of a shape. For rectilinear shapes (shapes made of straight lines), add the lengths of all the sides.
Perimeter of Rectilinear Shapes
Rectangle:
  • The perimeter of a rectangle is calculated using the formula:
  • Perimeter = 2 × (length + width)
Example:
  • Length = 5 cm, Width = 3 cm
  • Perimeter = 2 × (5 + 3) = 2 × 8 = 16 cm
Square:
  • The perimeter of a square is calculated using the formula:
  • Perimeter = 4 × side
Example:
  • Side = 4 cm
  • Perimeter = 4 × 4 = 16 cm
Area
The area is the space inside a shape. For rectilinear shapes, it is calculated by multiplying the length and width.
Area of Rectilinear Shapes
Rectangle:
  • The area of a rectangle is calculated using the formula:
  • Area = length × width
Example:
  • Length = 6 cm, Width = 4 cm
  • Area = 6 × 4 = 24 cm²

Square:
  • The area of a square is calculated using the formula:
  • Area = side × side
Example:
  • Side = 5 cm
  • Area = 5 × 5 = 25 cm²
Perimeter and Area of Compound Shapes
A compound shape is made up of more than one simple shape (e.g., rectangles or squares).

Perimeter:
  1. Add the lengths of all the outside sides.
  2. Example: A shape made of a rectangle with a square attached.
  3. Measure the outside sides and add them together.

Area:
  1. Break the compound shape into simpler shapes (like rectangles or squares), find the area of each, and then add them together.
  2. Example: A shape made of a rectangle and a smaller square.
  3. Find the area of the rectangle: length × width.
  4. Find the area of the square: side × side.
  5. Add them together.
Practical Example of Compound Shape
Let's calculate the perimeter and area of a compound shape.

Example Shape: A rectangle (6 cm × 4 cm) with a square (2 cm × 2 cm) attached on the side.

Step 1: Calculate the Perimeter
  1. Add up the outer sides of the shape.
  2. Perimeter = 6 + 4 + 6 + 2 + 2 = 20 cm.

Step 2: Calculate the Area
  1. Break the shape into a rectangle and a square.
  2. Rectangle area = 6 × 4 = 24 cm²
  3. Square area = 2 × 2 = 4 cm²
  4. Total area = 24 cm² + 4 cm² = 28 cm²
