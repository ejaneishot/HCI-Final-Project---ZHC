Multiplying and Dividing Large Numbers

Multiplying Large Numbers (Partitioning)
Break Down the Numbers (Partitioning):
Split the larger number into smaller parts. Multiply each part separately, then add the results.

Example:
  • 256 × 4: Break 256 into 200, 50, and 6.
  • Multiply: 200 × 4 = 800
  • 50 × 4 = 200
  • 6 × 4 = 24
  • Add: 800 + 200 + 24 = 1024.

Multiplying Large Numbers (Standard Algorithm)
Using the Standard Algorithm:
Write the larger number on top and the smaller number below it. Multiply each digit starting from the right.

Example:
  • 312 × 3:
  • 3 × 2 = 6
  • 3 × 1 = 3
  • 3 × 3 = 9
  • Final Answer: 936

Double-Digit Multiplication (Lattice Method)
Double-Digit Multiplication (Lattice Method):
Multiply each digit of the first number by each digit of the second number, then add.

Example:
  • 23 × 12:
  • 23 × 10 = 230
  • 23 × 2 = 46
  • Add: 230 + 46 = 276

Dividing Large Numbers (Estimation)
Estimate First:
Round the number to make division easier, then adjust.

Example:

  • Estimate: 720 ÷ 6 = 120
